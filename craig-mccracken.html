Biography

Craig McCracken was born in Charleroi, PA on March 31, 1971. Artist who created the immensely popular Cartoon Network animated television shows The Powerpuff Girls and Foster’s Home for Imaginary Friends. His work as a writer and storyboard artist for Dexter’s Laboratory shot him to fame. He influenced the animated work of Hayao Miyazaki. He started drawing at age three and never stopped. He studied at Cal Arts alongside fellow animator Genndy Tartakovsky . He was first hired by Hanna-Barbera Cartoons to serve as art director on 2 Stupid Dogs on TBS.
