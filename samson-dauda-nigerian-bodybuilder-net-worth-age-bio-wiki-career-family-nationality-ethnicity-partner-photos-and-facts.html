Professional Career of Professional Bodybuilder Samson Dauda

Samson Dauda first considered a career in sports. He enjoyed playing rugby as a child and went on to become a professional rugby player himself.

He did, however, frequently recommend that people try bodybuilding. Although he made the daring decision to leave sports and pursue professional bodybuilding after hearing it far too many times.

He quickly fell in love with the sport and learned a lot about it from the owner of a nearby gym. He was surprised when he won first place in his first competition.

Following this experience, he realized the vastness of the sport. As a result, he continued to compete in amateur events such as Olympia Amateur UK, Arnold Classic Europe, and others.

In 2017, he won his first major title in various categories at the Diamond Cup Rome. Following his success in amateur events, he was seen competing in pro-level competitions.

Two years later, he was denied gold in the Men’s Bodybuilding category at the British Grand Prix. He also finished fifth in the Portugal Pro the same year.

In 2021, he competed in the Arnold Classic UK Pro for the first time, finishing second. Following that, he was ranked fourth in the Arnold Classic 2022.

He also made his Mr Olympia debut the same year, competing among prominent personalities such as Chris Bumstead, Ramon Dino, and many others. Despite finishing sixth, he accomplished a tremendous performance considering it was his first.

He won gold in the Men’s Open category at the Arnold Classic in 2023. He also participated in the same category at Mr Olympia, finishing third, narrowly behind Derek Lunsford and Hadi Choopan.
