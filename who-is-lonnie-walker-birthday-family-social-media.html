Who Is Lonnie Walker?, Height, Birthday, Family, Social Media

Who Is Lonnie Walker?, Height, Birthday, Family, Social Media: Born on December 14, 1998, Lonnie Walker IV is an American professional basketball player.

He developed a love for playing basketball at a younger age and has been consistent throughout his career rising through the ranks to become one of the most sought-after basketball players.

At the time of this writing (Thursday, May 9, 2023), Walker is an American professional basketball player for the Los Angeles Lakers of the National Basketball Association.

He attended Reading Senior High School in Reading, Pennsylvania. In high school, he was named Mr. Pennsylvania Basketball and earned McDonald’s All-American honors

Walker played college basketball for the Miami Hurricanes. He made his collegiate debut with the team on November 10, 2017, recording 10 points and a season-high five assists.

Walker finished the season averaging 11.5 points per game and was named to the ACC all-freshman team. After his sole season with Miami Hurricanes ended, Walker declared for the 2018 NBA draft.

After being named to the all-freshman team in the Atlantic Coast Conference (ACC), Walker was selected in the first round of the 2018 NBA draft by the San Antonio Spurs.

On June 21, 2018, Walker was selected with the 18th overall pick by the San Antonio Spurs in the 2018 NBA draft and was later included in the 2018 NBA Summer League roster of the Spurs.

Walker made his NBA debut on January 3, 2019, in a 125–107 win against the Toronto Raptors, scoring three points and grabbing one rebound in five minutes of play

He played four seasons (2018–2022) with the San Antonio Spurs before signing with the Lakers. On July 6, 2022, Walker signed with the Los Angeles Lakers on a one-year, $6.5M contract.

He began the 2022–23 season playing in 32 games, all starts, before being sidelined beginning in late December due to an injury and later returned after missing 14 games

In May 2023, Lonnie Walker hit the headlines when he scored all 15 of his points in the fourth quarter to help his team (Lakers) overcome a seven-point deficit and win 104–101 over the visiting Golden State Warriors on Monday, May 8 during the Western Conference semi-finals.
